IKEv2 connection through NAT with NAT mapping change;

This test demonstrates how a change in NAT mapping, along the path,
can trigger a kernel mapping change and trigger MOBIKE, if enabled
on the connection.

Any inbound ESP from ROAD (behind the NAT) after the NAT gateway
remaps the port triggers a mapping change on EAST: EAST receives ESP
from the new port while outgoing packets still use the old port.

- ROAD establishes with EAST, a ping is exchanged.

- to simulate the mapping change, NIC remaps its NAT port.

- ROAD tries to send a further ping but, as expected, gets no response:

  With Linux, the failure is on the return path!

  Even though the kernel's states are configured for the OLD port it
  accepts the packet from the NEW PORT.  The ping reply goes back to
  the old PORT which the NAT drops.

- the broken traffic flow is verified on EAST and NIC

- EAST notices the kernel mapping change and initiates MOBIKE

- EAST sends an INFORMATIONAL with NAT-D payloads to ROAD on the new port

- ROAD responds with an INFORMATIONAL and updates its end

- EAST receives the response and updates its end

- time passes, liveness succeeds
